Code of Conduct

Standards for all members.

This page presents the conduct standards members are expected to follow. When the official attached document is provided, its final wording can replace this draft text.

Member Commitment

By joining Generation Justice, members agree to act respectfully, responsibly, and honestly in discussions, events, publications, competitions, internships, and country projects.

Apply for Membership

1. Respect and Professional Behaviour

Members must treat others with dignity, listen to different perspectives, and avoid abusive, discriminatory, or harassing conduct.

2. Honest Participation

Members should participate in good faith, provide accurate information, and avoid misrepresenting their role, qualifications, or project status.

3. Responsible Communication

Comments, articles, essays, broadcasts, and event contributions should be clear, lawful, respectful, and suitable for a professional Rule of Law project.

4. Academic Integrity

Members must submit original work, credit sources properly, and avoid plagiarism in competitions, magazine articles, and book submissions.

5. Confidentiality and Privacy

Members must not publish private information, confidential project materials, or personal data without permission.

6. Country Projects and Leadership

Members leading initiatives must act under project guidance, protect the reputation of the organization, and keep Trustees informed of country activity.

7. Moderation

Organizer-level accounts may remove comments that breach the Code of Conduct or undermine safe, serious, and constructive discussion.
